Chole Bhature

Description

Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish that combines two main components: Chole (spicy chickpea curry) and Bhature (deep-fried bread made from refined flour). It is a beloved street food and also commonly served in restaurants, especially in regions like Punjab, Delhi, and Uttar Pradesh.

Ingredient

For Chole
  • White chickpeas (kabuli chana) – 1 cup (soaked overnight)
  • Onion – 2, chopped
  • Tomato – 2, pureed
  • Ginger-garlic paste – 1 tbsp
  • Green chili – 1–2
  • Oil/ghee – 2–3 tbsp
  • Tea bags – 1–2 (optional, for color)
  • Spices: cumin seeds, turmeric, red chili, coriander powder, garam masala, amchur, chole masala, bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, black cardamom
  • Salt – to taste
  • Fresh coriander – for garnish
For Bhature
  • All-purpose flour (maida) – 2 cups
  • Semolina (sooji) – 2 tbsp (optional)
  • Yogurt – ½ cup
  • Baking soda – ¼ tsp
  • Baking powder – ½ tsp
  • Sugar – 1 tsp
  • Salt – ½ tsp
  • Oil – 1 tbsp (for dough) + for frying
  • Water – as needed

Instructions

  1. Boil chickpeas with salt and tea bags (optional) until soft.
  2. Heat oil, add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, and black cardamom.
  3. Sauté onions until golden, then add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies.
  4. Add tomato puree and cook till oil separates.
  5. Add spices (turmeric, chili, coriander powder, garam masala, amchur, chole masala) and mix well.
  6. Add boiled chickpeas and simmer for 10–15 minutes
  7. Garnish with coriander leaves.
  8. Mix flour, semolina, yogurt, baking soda, baking powder, sugar, salt, and oil.
  9. Add water gradually and knead a soft dough. Rest for 2–3 hours.
  10. Divide into balls, roll into discs.
  11. Deep-fry in hot oil until golden and puffed.

Serve hot with sliced onions, lemon, and pickle. Let me know if you want a one-pot version or tips for soft bhature!
