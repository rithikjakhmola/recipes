Chole Bhature

Description

Shahi Paneer is a popular North Indian royal dish made with soft cubes of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) simmered in a creamy, aromatic, and mildly spiced gravy of tomatoes, onions, cashew nuts, and traditional Indian spices. The term "Shahi" means "royal," and this dish truly lives up to its name with its rich texture and luxurious flavor. Often garnished with cream and fresh coriander, Shahi Paneer is typically served with naan, roti, or jeera rice, making it a favorite in festive and special meals.

Ingredient

For Chole
  • 200g paneer (cottage cheese), cubed
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, pureed
  • 10-12 cashew nuts, soaked and ground to a paste
  • 1 tablespoon fresh cream
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1-2 green cardamoms
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Water as needed

Instructions

  1. Heat ghee or oil in a pan and add cumin seeds, cardamoms, and cinnamon. Sauté for a few seconds.
  2. Add finely chopped onions and cook until golden brown.
  3. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1–2 minutes until the raw smell goes away.
  4. Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  5. Mix in the turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook for another minute.
  6. Add the cashew nut paste and cook for 3–4 minutes, stirring constantly to avoid sticking.
  7. Add water as needed to adjust the consistency and bring the gravy to a gentle boil.
  8. Add paneer cubes and simmer for 5–6 minutes so they absorb the flavor.
  9. Stir in the cream and garam masala. Mix well and cook for another 2 minutes.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
